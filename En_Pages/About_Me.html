About Me

Hello, I am Yunus Emre Özgel. I graduated from Bursa Technical University with a degree in Mechatronics Engineering, completing my studies with a 3.49/4.00 GPA and graduating with faculty honors. Throughout my education, I focused not only on learning engineering fundamentals, but also on understanding how to apply them to real industrial problems.

My professional experience is mainly focused on industrial automation, PLC programming, industrial robot programming, HMI/SCADA development, and machine commissioning. By working in real production environments, I have been actively involved in automation software development, system testing, commissioning, troubleshooting, and process optimization activities.

In my projects, I have developed control software using Siemens PLC platforms, designed HMI screens for operator panels, implemented alarm management structures, and built process control scenarios. I have also gained hands-on experience with ABB and KUKA industrial robots, including robot programming, TCP calibration, path optimization, cycle time improvement, and robot–PLC communication.

My responsibilities have not been limited to software development. I have also taken part in field commissioning, system validation, customer acceptance processes, and production performance improvement. These experiences have shown me that an automation system should not only operate correctly, but also be reliable, maintainable, traceable, and user-friendly for long-term production use.

My engineering approach is based on clean and understandable software structure, proper documentation, systematic problem solving, and effective communication within the project team. I see each project not only as a technical task, but as an integrated engineering solution that directly affects production quality, efficiency, safety, and process stability.

To support my professional development, I continuously follow emerging technologies, attend technical training programs, and work on personal engineering projects. My main areas of interest include robotic systems, smart manufacturing technologies, Industry 4.0 applications, digital factories, and automation software development.

This portfolio has been created to present the projects I have contributed to, my technical background, and my engineering mindset. My goal is to keep improving my technical expertise in robotics and industrial automation while contributing value to engineering teams, production systems, and industrial projects.

Engineering Approach

In engineering projects, I value analytical thinking, structured progress, and practical solutions that solve field problems permanently. I believe an automation system should be evaluated as a whole, including software, mechanics, electrical systems, safety infrastructure, operator interaction, and production requirements.

Career Objective

My career objective is to build deeper expertise in industrial robotics, PLC-based automation systems, and smart manufacturing technologies. In the long term, I aim to take an active role in automation solutions that make production lines more efficient, safer, more reliable, and more sustainable.
